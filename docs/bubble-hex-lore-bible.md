# BUBBLE HEX — Canon and Visual Bible

This document is the narrative contract for BUBBLE HEX. Runtime copy lives in
`app/game/content.ts`; changes to either source must be reflected in the other.

## Core promise

BUBBLE HEX is a camp neon arcade story with a dark fairy-tale heart. It is
fashionable, funny, eerie, emotionally sincere, non-graphic, and appropriate for
a teen audience. The game never treats trapped emotional echoes as living
victims: bubbles isolate them and a pop releases the memory safely.

Adult serpent-born sisters Vesper and Jade founded an after-dark club where no
guest would leave unseen. On opening night, the ageless impresario called the
Widow offered one perfect night forever. Vesper signed to save the club. Jade
saw the hidden clause and tore the contract to protect them. The incomplete vow
shattered the club into twelve chambers and split every shared memory into two
accusatory versions. The Widow survives on the sisters continuing to blame one
another.

The arcade loop tells this story. Bubbles isolate corrupted feelings. Chain
pops release linked memories. VENOM is the sisters' ancestral word for truth
held together under pressure. Jade Doors contain suppressed evidence. Clearing
the final bound echoes collapses the Black Bubble, expels the Widow, and lets
the sisters open their imperfect club at dawn.

## Character canon

### Vesper — Crimson Thorn

- Role: elder sister, showrunner, keeper of the stage flame.
- Desire: break the contract and release Jade from the endless night.
- Need: understand that protection without trust becomes control.
- Fear: abandonment after her confidence fails.
- Flaw: makes decisions for other people when frightened.
- Secret: read that the bargain had a price and believed she could outsmart it.
- Voice: clipped declarations, jokes at danger, plain apologies; never calls
  Jade weak.
- Shape language: thorn crown, hard shoulders, heart charm, tight tail coil.
- Motion: angular anticipation, sharp stops, proud vertical poses.
- Materials: black velvet, crimson lacquer, tarnished silver.
- Never: cruel for pleasure, armed with a conventional weapon, or reduced to
  the foolish sister.

### Jade — Glass Tide

- Role: younger sister, illusionist, secret archivist.
- Desire: restore the memories and expose the true bargain.
- Need: remain present when truth causes conflict.
- Fear: becoming another elegant manipulator.
- Flaw: withholds and disappears while calling it protection.
- Secret: hid the VENOM master key in the Jade Garden.
- Voice: precise imagery, dry observations, meaningful pauses; never lies
  outright.
- Shape language: veil fins, glass fangs, open sleeves, long tidal tail.
- Motion: circular gestures, flowing follow-through, weightless recovery.
- Materials: stained glass, jade silk, pearl silver.
- Never: the fragile sister, the Widow's willing heir, or morally correct by
  default merely because she resisted the contract.

### The Widow — Last Patron

- Role: ageless impresario and beneficiary of the broken vow.
- Desire: keep the sisters estranged so the Black Bubble sustains her.
- Fear: a sincere apology freely accepted.
- Flaw: cannot comprehend love that survives disappointment.
- Secret: showed each sister a different final clause to stage their fight.
- Voice: warm hospitality with predatory precision; never shouts.
- Shape language: cracked-heart torso, tarnished crown, spider couture, veil.
- Motion: smooth gliding interrupted by predatory single-frame snaps.
- Materials: black lacquer, cracked porcelain, old silver.
- Never: graphic, accidentally sympathetic, or powerful without distorted
  consent.

### Emotional echoes

Heartlings are discarded crushes. Velvet Bats are rumours hunting attention.
Mourning Eyes are tears held too long. Star Witches are performances denied a
final ovation. Stitch Dolls are names crossed off the guest list. Thorn Skulls
are promises stripped to the stubborn bone of their wording. They do not speak,
are readable without colour, and are always restored rather than killed.

## World progression

1. **Velvet Drain** — wet velvet, sapphire pipes, heart glass. The sisters
   rediscover their shared dream, two keys, and the Widow's invitation.
2. **Heartbreak Hotel** — mirrors, brass, faded carpet, Room 13. The divided
   clauses and the manufactured memory split are reconstructed.
3. **Jade Garden** — poison roses, mist, serpent glass. VENOM is revealed as a
   restoration key and Jade admits hiding it.
4. **Crimson Chapel** — wax, thorn iron, red glass. The staged fight and the
   contract's dependence on blame become undeniable.
5. **The Black Bubble** — void glass, broken neon, impossible grids. The
   original shared vow survives at the centre and can be reclaimed together.

## Reveal order

The twelve Jade Door fragments are immutable in this order: blueprint; shared
dream; Widow's invitation; Vesper signs; Jade tears the clause; memory split;
VENOM inheritance; Jade hides the key; Widow stages the fight; blame feeds the
contract; shared vow; opening at dawn. Normal stage copy may foreshadow a later
truth but must never confirm it early.

## Skins

Skins are cosmetic memory echoes, not alternate people or timelines. Crimson
Thorn and Glass Tide are the sisters' opening-night looks. Velvet Mourning and
Poison Current are Velvet Drain adaptations earned by clearing its third
chamber with the corresponding sister. A skin may change silhouette accents,
portrait, particles, and bubble colour; it must never change abilities,
collision, timing, personality, or canon history.

## Visual grammar

- Void black grounds every scene; each world owns one dominant neon colour.
- Heroes use clean, asymmetric silhouettes and pale eye highlights.
- Friendly or restorable magic uses round bubbles and linked curves.
- The Widow's influence uses hard cracks, contract lines, and spider geometry.
- Gameplay sprites stay crisp and high-contrast. Illustrated cards may carry
  velvet, glass, lacquer, condensation, and tarnished-metal detail.
- All readable text is rendered by the game, never baked into raster art.

## Voice and contradiction checklist

Before adding content, verify that it preserves all of the following:

- Both sisters contributed to the disaster and both acted from love.
- The Widow engineered incomplete knowledge; she did not mind-control them.
- Reconciliation requires truth and apology, not forgetting the harm.
- VENOM is restorative family magic, not poison or moral corruption.
- Popping an echo frees it.
- The normal ending opens the club at dawn; the true ending may warn that the
  hex can dream them back, but cannot invalidate their reconciliation.
- Comedy comes from theatrical confidence and arcade melodrama, never from
  mocking trauma, identity, or vulnerability.
